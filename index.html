About Me

Hi! I am a senior undergraduate student at Huazhong University of Science and Technology. Currently I am advised by Prof. Dawn Song at UC Berkeley as a research intern. I am broadly interested in various topics in security and privacy, especially the intersection of applied cryptography, machine learning, blockchain. In my undergraduate years, I worked a lot on provably secure machine learning and solving traditional security problems with AI. I am fortunate to work with wonderful people including Prof. Neil Gong at Duke University, Prof. Chao Zhang at Tsinghua University and Prof. Binghui Wang at Illinois Institute of Technology. I am applying for CS PhD in US starting from Fall 2023.

Projects

CoLink, a simple, secure, and flexible decentralized programming abstraction

CoLink contains:

  • A concise interface design for the orchestration of decentralized protocols.

  • A secure gateway server implementation that facilitates decentralized protocol executions.

  • SDKs in multiple languages for writing applications or cryptographic protocols that involve multiple participants.

  • Many existing protocols built on CoLink that extend its functionality and improve its usability.
